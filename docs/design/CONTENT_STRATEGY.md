# Content Strategy

## Purpose

This document defines the information architecture, content hierarchy, messaging strategy, and writing guidelines for the portfolio website.

The goal is to present work professionally while making it easy for recruiters, internship reviewers, and potential clients to understand the developer's skills and projects.

The content should remain authentic, concise, and focused on demonstrating practical abilities through real work.

---

# Content Principles

Every section should answer one important question for the visitor.

Avoid unnecessary text.

Show evidence instead of making unsupported claims.

Write clearly.

Prioritize readability.

---

# Target Audience

Primary

- Recruiters
- Hiring Managers
- Internship Reviewers

Secondary

- Developers
- Potential Clients
- Students

Every page should provide value to these audiences.

---

# Tone of Voice

The writing style should be:

- Professional
- Confident
- Clear
- Friendly
- Honest
- Direct

Avoid:

- Buzzwords
- Exaggeration
- Clickbait
- Marketing language
- Unnecessary emojis

# Content Review Checklist

Before publishing any page verify:

✓ Content is accurate.

✓ Grammar is correct.

✓ Headings are meaningful.

✓ Links work correctly.

✓ Content reflects current skills.

✓ No placeholder text remains.

✓ No duplicate information.

✓ Tone remains professional.

---

# Final Goal

Every visitor should be able to answer these questions within a few minutes:

- Who is the developer?
- What technologies does she work with?
- What projects has she built?
- What is her current focus?
- How can she be contacted?

If the website answers these questions clearly, the content strategy has achieved its purpose.
